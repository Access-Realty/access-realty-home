# Access Realty — Persona Landing Page Playbook

## Purpose

This document defines the process for creating 10 unique landing pages, each targeting a specific psychological profile of Access Realty user. These pages speak to the *real* motivation behind the purchase decision, not just the functional value of flat-fee listing.

Claude Code in the `access-realty-home` repo is responsible for building these pages.

---

## Phase 1: Define the Page Architecture

### 1.1 URL Structure

Each persona gets a dedicated route:

```
/for/smart-sellers           → Profile 2: The Engineer Who Hates Inefficient Markets
/for/investors                → Profile 3: The "I'm a Real Investor Now" First-Timer
/for/starting-over            → Profile 4: The Control Reclamation Widow/Widower
/for/independent-agents       → Profile 5: The Agent Quietly Quitting
/for/family-home              → Profile 6: The Adult Child Proving Competence
/for/fire                     → Profile 7: The FIRE Movement Optimizer
/for/your-way                 → Profile 8: The Realtor's Spouse
/for/next-chapter             → Profile 9: The Retired Professional Who Needs a Project
/for/experienced-sellers      → Profile 10: The "I Know Better" Seller
/for/fresh-start              → Profile 1: The Divorce Power Move Seller
```

Note: URLs are emotionally neutral and SEO-friendly. The persona targeting happens in the *content*, not the URL. Nobody should feel "called out" by the path they landed on.

### 1.3 Shared React Component

Create a single `PersonaLandingPage` component that accepts a persona config object:

```typescript
// src/types/persona-landing.ts

interface PersonaLandingConfig {
  slug: string;
  meta: {
    title: string;
    description: string;
    ogImage?: string;

    headline: string;

    subhead: string;

    ctaLink: string;
```

## Phase 2: Write the Persona Content

### Content Writing Rules

1. **Never name the persona directly.** The reader should feel *recognized*, not *categorized*. The page should feel like it was written by someone who understands them — not someone who profiled them.

2. **Lead with their worldview, not your product.** The first thing they read should make them think "yes, exactly." Only after that recognition moment do you introduce Access Realty.

3. **The headline does the heaviest lifting.** It should pass the "would I screenshot this and send it to someone?" test. It should articulate something they've felt but haven't said out loud.

4. **Match the emotional register.** The FIRE person wants data density. The widow/widower wants warmth and dignity. The divorce seller wants sharpness and victory language. Each page should *feel* different even though the structure is identical.

5. **The CTA should echo the identity, not the action.** Instead of "List Your Home" (generic), use language that reinforces who they are: "Take Control," "Keep Your Equity," "Do It Your Way."

6. **Objection handling should feel like mind-reading, not FAQ.** Frame objections as things they're thinking right now, not generic concerns.

### Content Specifications Per Persona

---

#### Profile 1: The Divorce Power Move Seller
**Route:** `/for/fresh-start`
**Emotional core:** Control, competence, victory
**Headline direction:** About handling it yourself, keeping what's yours, making smart moves during hard transitions
**Mirror statements:** You're already managing attorneys and mediators and custody schedules. The last thing you need is to hand 6% of your equity to someone for putting a sign in the yard. You want this done right, done clean, and done without anyone else taking a cut of what you built.
**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as the tool that lets them handle the sale on their terms — efficiently, with full visibility, keeping maximum proceeds
**Calculator preset:** Mid-range home ($350K–$450K), show savings prominently
**CTA tone:** Sharp, empowering: "Keep what's yours"
**Objection themes:** "Can I really do this while dealing with everything else?" / "What if my ex/attorney objects?"
**Visual mood:** Clean, minimal, strong typography. No softness. No stock photos of happy families.

---

#### Profile 2: The Engineer Who Hates Inefficient Markets
**Route:** `/for/smart-sellers`
**Emotional core:** Intellectual satisfaction, market correction, being right
**Headline direction:** About the math not making sense, about paying for value not tradition
**Mirror statements:** You've done the math. 5-6% of a home's value for ~40 hours of work is $200-400/hour. You've looked at what agents actually do and compared it to what they charge. The spread is indefensible. You're not cheap — you just refuse to pay irrational prices.
**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as the rational market correction they've been waiting for — the tool that aligns cost with actual value delivered
**Calculator preset:** Show hourly-rate breakdown of traditional commission vs. flat fee
**CTA tone:** Logical, satisfying: "Pay for value, not tradition"
**Objection themes:** "Is the MLS listing truly equivalent?" / "What services am I giving up?"
**Visual mood:** Data-forward. Show the math. Charts welcome. Clean, almost dashboard-like aesthetic.

---

#### Profile 3: The "I'm a Real Investor Now" First-Timer
**Route:** `/for/investors`
**Emotional core:** Identity formation, legitimacy, belonging
**Headline direction:** About having the same tools the pros use, about building your portfolio the smart way from day one
**Mirror statements:** You just closed on your first investment property. You're reading BiggerPockets, running numbers on the next deal, and thinking about scale. You know the investors who build real wealth don't give away 6% every time they move a property.
**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as what serious investors use — the professional-grade platform that treats them like the business operator they're becoming
**Calculator preset:** Lower price point ($200K–$300K), show per-deal savings and projected savings over 5 deals
**CTA tone:** Aspirational, insider: "List like a pro"
**Objection themes:** "I'm new — do I need a full-service agent to avoid mistakes?" / "Will buyer agents still show my property?"
**Visual mood:** Professional, substantial. Dashboard screenshots. Make the platform feel like a cockpit, not a discount tool.

---

#### Profile 4: The Control Reclamation Widow/Widower
**Route:** `/for/starting-over`
**Emotional core:** Autonomy, capability, dignity
**Headline direction:** About doing this your way, about being supported without being managed, about capability during a hard chapter
**Mirror statements:** Everyone means well. Your kids want to help. Your friends have opinions. Your spouse's agent wants to "take care of everything." But you need to do this — not because no one else can, but because proving you can matters right now more than almost anything.
**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as the tool that supports without taking over — you're in the driver's seat, with expert support one call away if you want it. Emphasis on "you decide when and if to ask for help."
**Calculator preset:** Mid-to-upper range home ($400K+), but savings are secondary — emphasize the process clarity
**CTA tone:** Warm, empowering: "You've got this. We've got your back."
**Objection themes:** "What if I get stuck on something?" / "Is there someone I can call?"
**Visual mood:** Warm, clear, spacious. Light color palette. Generous whitespace. Nothing cluttered or overwhelming. Every element should feel calming and organized.

#### Profile 5: The Agent Quietly Quitting
**Route:** `/for/independent-agents`
**Emotional core:** Exit strategy, freedom, testing a new identity
**Headline direction:** About keeping your license and your freedom, about listing your own properties without brokerage overhead
**Mirror statements:** You got into real estate for the independence. Somewhere along the way it became desk fees and mandatory meetings and someone else's brand on your work. You still have your license. You still close deals — especially your own. You just don't need the rest of it anymore.
**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as the bridge — full MLS access, transaction tools, no brokerage politics. Let them operate independently without formally "quitting."
**Calculator preset:** Show savings vs. brokerage split on their own deals
**CTA tone:** Quiet, knowing: "Your license. Your deals. Your way."
**Objection themes:** "Can I legally list through a flat-fee service while licensed?" / "What about E&O coverage?"
**Visual mood:** Understated, professional. Not "startup energy." More like a quiet, well-designed tool. Feels like a secret weapon, not a billboard.

#### Profile 6: The Adult Child Proving Competence

Not just the sale, but the way you manage it. You want them to see that you researched the options, made the smart call, and saved the family real money.

**CTA tone:** Responsible, confident: "Make the smart call for your family"
**Objection themes:** "My parents' agent wants the listing — how do I explain this?" / "What if something goes wrong and the family blames me?"

#### Profile 7: The FIRE Movement Optimizer
**Route:** `/for/fire`
**Emotional core:** Optimization, savings rate protection, identity consistency
**Headline direction:** About keeping your savings rate intact on the biggest transaction of your life, about not undoing years of optimization with one lazy default

**Reframe:** Position Access Realty as the obvious move for anyone who optimizes — frame the commission savings as X months/years closer to their FIRE date
**Calculator preset:** Show savings converted to: months of expenses covered, additional investment growth at 7% over 10 years, FIRE date acceleration
**CTA tone:** Precise, data-driven: "Optimize the biggest line item"
**Objection themes:** "Is this actually the same MLS listing?" / "Time vs. money — is the DIY effort worth it?"
**Visual mood:** Minimal, almost spreadsheet-clean. Numbers prominent. No fluff. Feels like a Mr. Money Mustache blog post turned into a product page.

**CTA tone:** Direct, peer-level: "You know your market. Now list in it."
**Objection themes:** "Do I get full MLS control or does someone else manage my listing?" / "Can I update my listing myself?"
**Visual mood:** No-nonsense. Feels like a power-user tool. Feature-dense hero. No "let us help you" energy — more like "here are the keys."

## Phase 3: Build the Pages

### 3.1 Claude Code Implementation Prompt

When ready to build, feed Claude Code the following instruction set along with this document:

```
CONTEXT:
I'm building persona-specific landing pages for Access Realty.
Refer to the attached playbook (this document) for full content specs.

TECH STACK:
- Vite + React + TypeScript (existing repo: access-realty-home)
- Tailwind CSS for styling
- React Router for routing

IMPLEMENTATION STEPS:

1. Create the shared type definition:
   src/types/persona-landing.ts
   (Use the PersonaLandingConfig interface from the playbook)

2. Create the shared layout component:
   src/components/landing/PersonaLandingPage.tsx
   - Accepts a PersonaLandingConfig prop
   - Renders all sections from the shared component structure
   - Each section is its own sub-component for maintainability:
     - HeroSection.tsx
     - MirrorSection.tsx
     - ReframeSection.tsx
     - ProofSection.tsx (includes savings calculator)
     - HowItWorksSection.tsx
     - ObjectionSection.tsx
     - FinalCtaSection.tsx

3. Create the savings calculator component:
   src/components/landing/SavingsCalculator.tsx
   - Accepts preset values from persona config
   - Interactive: user can adjust home price
   - Shows flat-fee vs. traditional commission comparison
   - Profile 7 (FIRE) variant also shows compound growth of savings

4. Create 10 persona config files:
   src/data/personas/fresh-start.ts
   src/data/personas/smart-sellers.ts
   src/data/personas/investors.ts
   src/data/personas/starting-over.ts
   src/data/personas/independent-agents.ts
   src/data/personas/family-home.ts
   src/data/personas/fire.ts
   src/data/personas/your-way.ts
   src/data/personas/next-chapter.ts
   src/data/personas/experienced-sellers.ts

5. Register routes:
   Add /for/:persona routes that load the correct config
   and render PersonaLandingPage

6. Style variations:
   Each persona config includes a backgroundVariant string.
   Map these to Tailwind theme overrides:
   - "sharp"      → dark bg, high contrast, strong type (profiles 1, 10)
   - "data"       → light bg, monospace accents, chart-friendly (profiles 2, 7)
   - "warm"       → soft palette, generous spacing, rounded elements (profiles 4, 8)
   - "pro"        → navy/slate, dense layout, enterprise feel (profiles 3, 5, 9)
   - "trust"      → blue/green, clean, financial advisor aesthetic (profile 6)

QUALITY CHECKS:
- [ ] Each page passes Lighthouse accessibility audit
- [ ] Calculator works with keyboard navigation
- [ ] All CTAs have tracking-ready data attributes (data-persona, data-section, data-action)
- [ ] Meta tags / OG tags are populated per persona for social sharing
- [ ] Mobile-responsive: hero stacks cleanly, calculator is touch-friendly
- [ ] No persona page ever says "flat fee" in the headline — lead with identity, not price
```

### 3.2 Build Order

Build in this sequence to minimize rework:

1. **Shared components first** — PersonaLandingPage layout, SavingsCalculator, all section sub-components
2. **One persona end-to-end** — Pick Profile 2 (Engineer) as the pilot. It's the most straightforward content and the data-forward aesthetic is easy to validate.
3. **Style variants** — Build all 5 visual themes against the pilot page
4. **Remaining 9 personas** — Content population into config files. Each should take ~15 minutes once the scaffold is solid.
5. **Calculator variants** — FIRE compound growth variant, investor multi-deal projection variant
6. **Routing and meta tags** — Wire up all routes, populate OG tags
7. **Analytics hooks** — Add data attributes for tracking

| 10 — Expert Seller | Google Ads: "flat fee MLS listing", "list home without agent" | Already searching for the solution |

### 4.2 UTM Tagging Convention

Every link pointing to a persona page should follow:

```
https://accessrealty.com/for/{slug}?utm_source={source}&utm_medium={medium}&utm_campaign=persona-{slug}
```

This lets you measure which persona pages convert and at what rate, independently from the main site.

### 4.3 A/B Testing Priority

Test these first — they have the highest expected impact:

1. **Hero headline** — Test identity-forward vs. savings-forward for each persona
2. **Calculator position** — Test above-the-fold vs. after the mirror section
3. **CTA language** — Test persona-specific vs. generic across all pages
4. **Social proof type** — Test testimonial vs. stat vs. savings counter

## Phase 5: Measurement

### What "Working" Looks Like

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Time on page | >2 min average | They're reading, not bouncing — the mirror is working |
| Calculator engagement | >40% of visitors interact | The savings are becoming personal and concrete |
| CTA click-through | >5% | The identity pitch is converting to action |
| Scroll depth | >70% reach final CTA | The page narrative holds attention through the full arc |
| Conversion to listing | Track per persona | Identifies which profiles have real commercial value vs. theoretical interest |

### Kill Criteria

If a persona page gets 500+ visitors and converts at <1% to any meaningful action (calculator use, CTA click, form start), either the content missed the emotional target or the profile doesn't represent real demand. Rewrite the mirror section first — that's where recognition either happens or fails.
