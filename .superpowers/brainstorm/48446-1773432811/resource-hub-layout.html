Selling Resources Hub — Organization

How should visitors find and browse these 11 resource pages?

A

Single Flat List

All 11 resources in one clean grid — no categories. Simple, scannable. Each card shows title, thumbnail (video poster or illustration), and a short description.

Pros

  • Simple to build and maintain
  • Easy to add new resources later
  • Works great at 11 items

Cons

  • No topical grouping — harder to browse by need
  • Less SEO juice from category pages
B

Grouped by Category

Resources organized under 3-4 category headings on one page. E.g., "Preparing Your Home" (photography, lockbox, square footage), "Pricing & Finances" (pricing strategy, capital gains, lender repairs), "Showings & Access" (access guide, pets), "Legal & Disclosures" (disclosure, survey, conveyance).

Pros

  • Visitors can jump to their concern
  • Groups create a natural learning path
  • Categories can become SEO landing pages later

Cons

  • Some topics could fit multiple categories
  • More structure to maintain
C

Selling Timeline (Journey-Based)

Resources ordered by when a seller needs them: "Before You List" → "Preparing Your Home" → "While It's Listed" → "Under Contract". Tells a story and guides sellers through the process.

Pros

  • Matches how sellers actually think
  • Creates a natural content journey
  • Strong brand credibility — feels like a guided experience
  • Great for SEO pillar page strategy

Cons

  • Harder to slot new topics that don't fit the timeline
  • Some topics span multiple phases
