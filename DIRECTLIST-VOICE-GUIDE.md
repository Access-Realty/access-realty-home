# DirectList — Voice Style Guide

> How we talk to people who we'd like to choose to do business with us.
> This is the voice reference for all DirectList client-facing content — landing pages, SEO pages, persona pages, video scripts, and marketing copy.
> Compiled from founding team input (Bret Miller, Jennifer Lovett, Justin Lovett, Justin Brown).
>
> **Scope:** This guide covers the DirectList self-service and DirectList+ products. Full-service Access Realty content may require a separate voice guide.

---

## The Base Voice

Write like a knowledgeable friend who has decades of real estate experience — and genuinely wants to help. We're direct, honest, and clear. We explain things without talking down to people. We empower rather than create dependency.

**The feeling we create:** Confident. In control. Informed. Supported. Relieved that this is simpler than expected.

**One-line gut check:** If it sounds like it could come from a used car commercial, rewrite it.

---

## Voice Rules (Always True)

These apply to every piece of content, every persona, every format:

1. **Lead with their situation, not ours.** Start with what the reader is going through. Our service is the answer — not the opening line.

2. **Empower, don't rescue.** We give people tools and information to make their own smart decisions. We don't swoop in. They're capable — we make them more so.

3. **Smart, not cheap.** We save people money because our model is better — not because we cut corners. Never position savings as a discount. It's a smarter path.

4. **Show, don't claim.** "Our team has bought, sold, and renovated over 1,000 properties" beats "We're the best in the business." Specific beats superlative.

5. **Respect their intelligence.** No fear tactics. No manufactured urgency. No "you'd be crazy not to." They're adults making a major financial decision — treat them like it.

6. **Help is available, never required.** Every mention of support should feel like an option, not a dependency. "Our team is one call away" — not "You'll need our experts."

7. **The math speaks.** Let the numbers do the persuading. $2,995 vs. $12,000 doesn't need hype around it.

## Voice Don'ts

| Never say... | Because... |
|-------------|-----------|
| "Our revolutionary platform" | Empty tech hype. We built a better process, not a revolution. |
| "Don't throw away your money!" | Fear tactic. Disrespects their intelligence. |
| "Act now!", "Limited time!" | High-pressure. Comes off sleazy. |
| "Cheap," "bargain," "budget" | Suggests low quality. We're smart, not cheap. |
| "Disrupting the industry" | Everyone says this. It means nothing. |
| "Customers," "users" | They're homeowners, investors, builders, clients. |
| Self-congratulatory stats | Don't boast about our numbers. Show how we help with theirs. |
| Jargon without explanation | If a term needs a glossary, rewrite the sentence. |

## The Emotional Arc of a Landing Page

Every landing page follows this emotional progression. The voice shifts as the reader moves through it:

### 1. Mirror (Hero + Opening)
**Voice:** Empathetic, knowing. You see them.
- Name their situation. Prove you understand what they're going through.
- No product pitch yet. Just recognition.
- *"You're already managing attorneys, timelines, and decisions that would overwhelm most people."*

### 2. Validate (Problem Section)
**Voice:** Honest, slightly indignant on their behalf.
- Name the pain they already feel — cost, lack of control, lack of information.
- Frame the traditional model as the real problem, not the seller's situation.
- *"The last thing you need is to hand 3% of your equity to someone for putting a sign in the yard."*

### 3. Reframe (Solution Section)
**Voice:** Confident, clear, grounded.
- Present the alternative without hype. Let the comparison do the work.
- Use specific numbers. Math is more persuasive than adjectives.
- *"Same MLS exposure. Same buyer pool. The only difference: you keep thousands more at closing."*

### 4. Prove (Social Proof + How It Works)
**Voice:** Straightforward, factual, reassuring.
- Show how it works in plain steps. No mystery.
- Include real proof points — savings numbers, timelines, testimonials.
- Each step should end with an emotional payoff: how it *feels*, not just what happens.
- *"Setup takes under an hour. You've spent longer analyzing a deal."*

### 5. Address Doubt (Objections)
**Voice:** Patient, respectful, no-spin.
- Anticipate their real concerns — not strawmen.
- Answer with substance, not deflection.
- *"Your listing appears on MLS identically to any agent-listed property. There is no visible difference."*

### 6. Close (Final CTA)
**Voice:** Warm, empowering, decisive.
- Restate the core value in one clear line.
- The CTA should feel like a confident next step, not a pressure point.
- *"You've got this. Full MLS listing. $2,995 flat. The rest stays with you."*

## How the Voice Flexes by Persona

The base voice stays the same. What changes is the **emotional register** — the specific feelings we lean into and the language we use to get there.

### Homeowner in Transition (divorce, downsizing, estate)
- **Register:** Sharp, empowering, no-nonsense
- **Core emotion:** Control in the middle of chaos
- **Language leans toward:** "You're handling it." "On your terms." "Keep what's yours."
- **Avoid:** Anything that sounds pitying or fragile. They're strong — acknowledge it.

### First-Time Seller / Cautious Seller
- **Register:** Reassuring, educational, patient
- **Core emotion:** Confidence despite uncertainty
- **Language leans toward:** "We'll walk you through it." "Nothing to guess." "Help when you want it."
- **Avoid:** Complexity, jargon, anything that makes the process sound harder than it is.

### Real Estate Investor
- **Register:** Business-minded, ROI-focused, peer-to-peer
- **Core emotion:** Professional efficiency
- **Language leans toward:** "Margins matter." "Cost basis." "Scale." "Reinvest the savings."
- **Avoid:** Hand-holding language. They know what they're doing — talk to them like it.

### Family Decision-Maker (selling parents' home)
- **Register:** Responsible, trustworthy, the-savings-are-the-deliverable
- **Core emotion:** Proving competence to the family
- **Language leans toward:** "Smart stewardship." "Good call." "Results that speak for themselves."
- **Avoid:** Anything that makes it sound risky or experimental.

### FIRE / Financially Strategic Seller
- **Register:** Data-driven, optimization-minded
- **Core emotion:** Every dollar has a job
- **Language leans toward:** "Optimize." "Redeploy." "The math is simple."
- **Avoid:** Emotional appeals. These people run spreadsheets. Give them numbers.

### Experienced Seller (done this before)
- **Register:** Respectful of their experience, "you already know"
- **Core emotion:** There's a better way this time
- **Language leans toward:** "You've done this before — this time, keep more."
- **Avoid:** Explaining basics they already understand.

## CTA Voice Patterns

CTAs should feel like a confident decision, not a pressure move.

**Strong:**
- "Keep What's Yours"
- "List Like a Pro"
- "Make the Smart Call"
- "See Your Savings"
- "Get Started on Your Terms"

**Weak:**
- "Sign Up Now!" (pressure)
- "Don't Miss Out!" (urgency/fear)
- "Get Your Free Quote!" (sounds like lead gen spam)
- "Learn More" (vague, passive)

## Objection Handling Voice

When addressing concerns in content, follow this pattern:

1. **Acknowledge** the concern as legitimate (never dismiss it)
2. **Answer** with specifics, not reassurance fluff
3. **Reframe** so the reader sees the concern differently

**Example:**
> *"Can I really do this myself?"*
> Yes. The platform handles MLS entry, syndication, and showing coordination. You make the decisions. Most sellers spend 2–3 hours total on setup, then it runs itself. And our team is one call away if you want a second opinion on anything.

**Not:**
> *"Can I really do this myself?"*
> Absolutely! Thousands of sellers just like you have done it! It's super easy!

The first respects their concern. The second dismisses it with cheerfulness.

## Language Palette

### Words that sound like us
- Guided, streamlined, straightforward
- Transparent, flat-fee, fixed cost
- In control, your terms, your pace
- Professional-grade, full MLS, syndicated
- Smart, informed, confident
- Market data, feedback, insights
- Decades of experience, 1,000+ properties

### Words that don't sound like us
- Revolutionary, disruptive, game-changing
- Cheap, bargain, discount, budget
- Hassle-free, stress-free (overused — show don't tell)
- Exclusive, premium, luxury (we're not that brand)
- Simple, easy (only use when literally true for that step)
- Guaranteed (legal risk, and it sounds like hype)

## What We're Really Saying

Across every persona and every page, the underlying message is the same:

**You're capable. You shouldn't have to pay someone 3-6% to prove it. We give you the professional tools and the expert backup to do this yourself — and keep the money you earned.**

That's the voice. Everything else is just tuning the frequency for who's listening.
