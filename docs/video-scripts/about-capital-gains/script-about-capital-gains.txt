Capital Gains Tax When Selling a Home Owned Less Than Two Years


When selling a home you have owned for less than two years, capital gains tax may apply. This is general information only and not legal or financial advice. Consult a CPA or attorney for guidance specific to your situation.
Capital gains is the profit you make after subtracting what you paid for the home, plus improvements and selling costs.
The IRS has a rule that lets you avoid this tax if you owned and lived in the home for at least two of the last five years.

If you sell before meeting that rule, the exclusion usually does not apply.
If you owned the home for less than one year, the profit is taxed like regular income.

If you owned it for more than one year but less than two, it is taxed at long-term capital gains rates.
Texas does not have a state income tax, but federal taxes may still apply.

In some cases, a partial tax break may be available for job changes, health issues, or other unexpected events.
Talking with a tax professional or attorney before selling is always a smart move.
