Selling Your Home When You Have Pets
Selling a home with pets is very common, but it may require extra preparation. Here are some tips we recommend when selling a home with pets.
For showings, removing pets from the home is the most recommended option. This allows buyers to walk through the home without distractions and focus on how the space would feel for them. Even calm, well behaved pets can be a distraction, which may shorten a showing and take attention away from the home itself.
If removing pets is not possible, they should be placed in a crate. For the pet’s comfort and safety, using a breathable cover over the crate can help reduce stress and discourage buyers from approaching the crate during showings. Any crated pet must be kept in the same location every time, exactly as noted in the listing instructions. This information is shared with the showing agent prior to the appointment so they know what to expect.
Pets should never be loose in the backyard. Buyers expect to view the entire property, including outdoor areas, and should be able to do so safely and comfortably.
Odors are one of the biggest concerns for buyers. Professional carpet and furniture cleaning is highly recommended, as pet odors often linger in furniture and fabrics, not just flooring. Avoid heavy candles or air fresheners, as buyers may assume something is being masked. If you are unsure about odors, asking someone outside the household for honest feedback can be helpful.
Finally, reduce pet related clutter. Store toys, bowls, leashes, and beds neatly so the home feels clean and clutter free.
Preparing the home in this way helps keep the focus where it should be, on the property itself.
