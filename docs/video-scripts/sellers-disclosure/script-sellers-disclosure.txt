What Is a Seller’s Disclosure and Why It’s Important:
Before you list your home, one of the most important documents you will complete is the Seller’s Disclosure Notice.
The Seller’s Disclosure is a legal document required in Texas for most previously occupied single-family homes. Its purpose is simple. You are disclosing what you know about the condition of the property so buyers can make an informed decision. 
This form is not a warranty and it is not a home inspection. It is a snapshot of your knowledge of the home based on your experience living there or owning it.
For buyers, the disclosure helps identify known issues with things like the roof, foundation, plumbing, electrical systems, or past water damage. This information helps buyers understand the property and avoid surprises later.
For sellers, the disclosure is just as important. Completing it accurately helps protect you from future claims of fraud or misrepresentation. Under Texas law, failing to disclose known issues can expose you to legal liability for hidden defects for up to four years after the sale has closed.
The form covers a wide range of topics, including major systems, past flooding or insurance claims, pest issues, repairs, additions, and whether there are any known conditions that could affect health or safety.
When filling it out, answer each question honestly and based on your actual knowledge. You are not required to investigate, guess, or disclose rumors from prior owners. If you do not know the answer, “Unknown” is an acceptable response.
A common rule to follow is this.
Do not overshare, but do not withhold known information. If you know about an issue, disclose it.
The Seller’s Disclosure must be delivered before or at the time the contract goes into effect. Providing it late can give the buyer the right to terminate the contract.
Taking the time to complete this form carefully helps set clear expectations, builds trust, and protects you legally throughout the transaction.
