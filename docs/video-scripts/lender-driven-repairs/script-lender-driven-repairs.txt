Additional Lender Required Repairs After The Appraisal


If your buyer is using an FHA or VA loan, there is something important to know about what can happen after the appraisal.
Even if the home appraises at the purchase price, the lender may still require certain repairs before the loan can close. These are called lender-required repairs.
With FHA and VA loans, the appraisal is not only about value. The lender also uses it to confirm the home meets their minimum property standards. If the appraiser identifies issues that do not meet those standards, the lender can require they be addressed before approving the loan.
Common lender-required repairs can include things like peeling or chipped paint, missing handrails, broken windows, roof issues, or electrical concerns. These requests come from the lender, not the buyer.
If lender-required repairs are issued, you generally have a few options. You can complete the repairs, offer a credit if the lender allows it, or decide not to move forward, which could mean losing the buyer.
It is important to understand that buyers using FHA or VA loans cannot waive these repairs, even if they want to. The lender makes the final decision.
The best way to prepare is to be aware of your buyer’s loan type and address obvious issues early when possible. Being prepared helps avoid delays and keeps the transaction moving forward.
