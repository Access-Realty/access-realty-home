Which Items Convey with the Property, and Which Do Not?


Before listing your home, it is important to understand what conveys with the property, meaning what stays and what goes.
In Texas, items that are permanently attached to the home are generally included in the sale, while movable items are not. A helpful way to think about this is to imagine turning the house upside down. Anything that would remain attached is typically included with the sale, while anything that would fall is generally not.
Attached items usually include light fixtures, ceiling fans, TV mounts and brackets, curtain rods, built-in appliances such as stoves, garage door openers, and landscaping planted in the ground.
Movable items, or personal property, usually include furniture, wall art, area rugs, freestanding shelves, and potted plants. Even large decorative potted plants are considered personal property unless they are planted in the ground.
One important tip.
If there is something attached to the home that is sentimental and you plan to keep it, such as a special light fixture or chandelier, the best option is to replace it before listing photos are taken. That way, the item you want to keep is never part of the listing, the photos, or the negotiation.
Now let’s address a common source of confusion.
In Texas, refrigerators typically do not convey unless they are specifically included. Sellers often advertise items like refrigerators, washers, or dryers as part of the sale, but here is what matters most.
The MLS is not a contract. It is an advertisement.
The contract is the final word on what stays or goes.
If an item is not clearly included or excluded in the contract in writing, it can create confusion or legal issues later.
The takeaway is simple.
If there is something you want to keep, or something you want to include, it must be clearly addressed in the contract. Being clear upfront helps avoid misunderstandings and keeps your transaction moving forward smoothly.
