Are you considering a leaseback?


A leaseback, also called a Seller’s Temporary Lease, allows you to sell your home and remain in it for a short period after closing. In Texas, this is handled using a standardized lease agreement and is limited to no more than 60 days.
Once the sale closes, you are no longer the homeowner. You become a tenant, and the buyer becomes your landlord. All terms are negotiated upfront, including the length of the stay, daily rent, security deposit, utilities, and the move out date.
The buyer carries homeowner’s insurance on the property, while you are required to carry renter’s insurance for your personal belongings and liability.
Rent and any security deposit are typically paid at closing and handled through the title company.
It is important to understand that not all buyers or their lenders allow leasebacks. Some lenders prohibit them entirely, and some buyers need possession immediately. As a result, requiring a leaseback will most likely limit your potential buyer pool and can prevent a transaction from moving forward.
A leaseback can be useful when your next home is not ready or you need extra time to move. However, as a general rule, we discourage sellers from requiring leasebacks unless they are truly necessary.
