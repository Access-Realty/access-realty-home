What Can I Include in Square Footage?
In this video, we are going to cover what can and cannot be included when listing the square footage of your home. Square footage is an important detail for buyers, but it is also an area where mistakes or misunderstandings can cause problems later. The goal is to be accurate, transparent, and clear.
Always Disclose Your Source
If you list the square footage of your property, you should always disclose where that number came from. This might be a prior appraisal, tax records, a survey, builder plans, or another source.
If you have any reason to believe the square footage from your source may not be accurate, that should be disclosed.
What Is Typically Included in Texas
In Texas, square footage generally includes interior living areas that are finished, enclosed, and part of the main structure of the home.
This means bedrooms, bathrooms, living areas, and other spaces that are heated and cooled.
Interior closets, entryways, utility rooms, and mud rooms are typically included as long as they are finished and part of the living space.
Enclosed patios may be included only if they are heated and cooled and similar in quality to the rest of the home.
Finished attic space may be included if it conforms to the original structure, is heated and cooled, and is finished to the same standard as the rest of the home.
What Is Not Included
Garages are not included unless they have been properly converted to living space that is heated and cooled. Screened patios, unfinished areas, and open second-floor spaces, such as areas above vaulted rooms or stairways, are also not included.
Detached spaces, like an office or guest room in a separate building, are measured separately and should not be combined with the home’s square footage.
Accurate square footage avoids misunderstandings and helps keep the sale moving forward without unnecessary issues.
