How To Prepare For Your Upcoming Open House


An open house is a great way to market your home and give potential buyers the opportunity to walk through the property in person. A licensed agent will be on site to host the open house and handle everything during the event.
To ensure the open house goes smoothly, there are a few important things to do ahead of time.
First, sellers should not be present during the open house. This allows buyers to feel comfortable walking through the home and asking questions freely.
One Week Before
About a week before the open house, do a depersonalization check. Put away personal photos, heirlooms, valuables, and sensitive personal items.
Plan to deep clean the home and create a simple routine to keep it tidy leading up to the open house. This is also a good time to tend to the yard by mowing, trimming bushes, and clearing walkways so the exterior makes a strong first impression.
One Day Before
The day before the open house, vacuum, mop, and dust throughout the home. Focus on kitchens, bathrooms, and floors.
Do a quick sniff test and take care of any odors. Clean pet areas thoroughly and air out the home if needed.
A Few Hours Before
A few hours before the open house, open windows if the weather allows to bring in fresh air. Turn on all lights so the home feels bright and welcoming.
Pets should be removed from the home during the open house. This is important for both buyer comfort and safety.
Secure personal documents, mail, and any sensitive information. Take out the trash and hide bins out of sight.
Put away everyday clutter such as shoes, jackets, and countertop items. Do one final walkthrough of each room to make sure everything is clean, orderly, and ready for visitors.
Taking these steps helps create a positive experience for buyers and allows the open house to showcase your home at its best.
