Preparing the Property for Photos


Preparing Your Home for Professional Photos
In this video, we will walk through how to prepare your home so your listing photos look great, attract buyers, and help prevent issues later in the process.
Professional photos are one of the most important parts of your listing. They are often the first impression buyers get, so a little preparation ahead of time can make a big difference.
Preparing Every Room
Start with a thorough cleaning. Dust, vacuum, mop, and wipe down surfaces throughout the home. Pay close attention to floors, windows, and mirrors so everything looks clean and well maintained.
Turn on all interior lights and replace any burnt-out bulbs. Open curtains and blinds to let in natural light. Bright rooms photograph better and feel more inviting.
Next, remove clutter and personal items. Clear off countertops, tables, and shelves. Put away family photos, paperwork, pet items, cords, trash cans, laundry baskets, and cleaning supplies. The goal is to help buyers focus on the space itself.
Kitchen and Bathrooms
These rooms get a lot of attention from buyers, so keep them simple.
In the kitchen, clear countertops or leave just one or two minimal decorative items. Clean appliances, sinks, and backsplashes until they shine.
In bathrooms, remove all toiletries, close toilet lids, hang clean neutral towels, and make sure mirrors and fixtures are spotless.
Living Areas and Bedrooms
Arrange furniture neatly and remove extra pieces that make rooms feel crowded. Make all beds and turn off televisions.
Exterior and Curb Appeal
The outside of your home matters just as much as the inside.
Mow the lawn, trim bushes, sweep walkways and driveways, and remove cars if possible. Clean exterior windows at least 48 hours before photos are taken so they have time to dry and won’t appear streaky. Hide trash cans, hoses, tools, and toys.
Pets and Access
Photography equipment can be stressful for pets, so plan to have them out of the home during the photo shoot.
Conveying vs. Non-Conveying Items
This is an important step many sellers overlook.
Anything attached to the home is generally assumed to stay with the property. This includes light fixtures, ceiling fans, and mounted items.
If there is something attached that you plan to take with you, especially a sentimental fixture, we recommend replacing it before photos are taken. Once an item appears in listing photos, buyers expect it to be included. Swapping it out ahead of time helps avoid confusion or negotiation issues later.
Before the photographer arrives, do one last walkthrough and look at your home from a buyer’s perspective. Taking these steps helps your photos accurately represent your home and sets your listing up for success.
