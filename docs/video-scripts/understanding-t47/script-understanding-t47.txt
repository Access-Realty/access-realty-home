What Is a T-47 and Why Do I Need One?
If you plan to use an existing survey when selling your home, you will be required to complete a document called a T-47, also known as the Residential Real Property Affidavit.
The T-47 is used when the buyer and title company rely on an existing survey instead of ordering a new one. This form allows you, as the seller, to confirm that the survey on file is still accurate.
The T-47 works together with the survey to help the title company determine what title insurance coverage they can provide. If a T-47 is not completed, the title company may require a new survey, which can add cost and delay to the transaction.
One of the most important sections of the T-47 asks for the date of the survey. This should be the date of the most recent survey of the property, whether it was ordered by you or a previous owner. Using a different date often results in the title company requesting a new survey.
The form also asks you to confirm whether any major changes have occurred to the property since that survey date. This includes things like new structures, additions, changes to boundary fences, or new easements. If there have been no changes, you can simply write “None.”
The T-47 is an affidavit, which means it must be signed in front of a notary. Do not sign it ahead of time. You will need to sign the form while the notary is present.
In short, the T-47 helps avoid the cost and delay of a new survey, but only if it is completed accurately and properly notarized.
